Why Responsive Web Design(RWD)?

You might ask yourself why RWD? Should I do it just because Google says so? Or because Google threatens to bury your site below mobile friendly sites? Ultimately decreasing your chances of ever being found. Which will be fine when you're forced to live in a van down by the river. Or is that Amazon's fault…

Luckily Google has given us some resources to help convert sites to mobile friendly RWD sites. Check out their resources here. But let's dig a little deeper into the reasons why RWD makes sense for you and your users.

Once upon a time there was a two-site strategy, build a desktop site and separate mobile site. One works perfect on desktops, the other looks great on mobile. The only problem is now you're in the business of maintaining two completely different sites and code. As if changes weren't hard enough, now you must worry about consistency and site changes on two platforms. Not to mention that the two sites won't necessarily work for the thousands of different devices that exist today. Devices are kind of like bunnies, you know if devices could reproduce…

Anyway, the RWD philosophy is a one site solution that usually adapts to a few different device sizes e.g. phones, tablets, and desktops. As the screen size decreases, it helps you edit down your core content to what's important. It also makes navigation, links, and text readable without having to zoom in. If you're not on board yet, here's a few other stats:

  • 67% of users say they are more likely to buy from a site that is mobile friendly✱
  • Mobile devices surpassed desktop users in viewing the internet globally in 2014, which is just over 4 years ago. That's like an eternity in cell phone designs.✱

In conclusion, build mobile first sites or convert your desktop sites to RWD sites. The alternative is that you might find your ever-increasing web audiences shrink to an audience of one, sooner than you think.
